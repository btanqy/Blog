Food

Yaoyue Teahouse


Rating: 6/10 | Price: $15-$24 | Location: Yaoyue Teahouse @ Maokong, Taipei
The food is pretty standard, nothing to write home about for the egg dish with tieguanyin leaves, edamame, stir friend squid with pork, or tofu. The tieguanyin sausage had an odd stewed pork flavor mixed with tea, whilst still having the essence of a taiwanese sausage.

Tieguanyin Tea Set


Rating: 6/10 | Price: $11 + $3pp | Location: Yaoyue Teahouse @ Maokong, Taipei
 Although it was great to experience how to make tea (pouring water into pot, and pot into pouring cup, and pouring cup into normal cup), the traditional tieguanyin leaves we ordered didn't really taste fragrant, and had a somewhat bitter aquafina-type aftertaste that I didn't enjoy. Take the time to travel to the Taiwan Tea Promotion Center instead, where they'll talk to you extensively about tea (we were there for like at least half an hour), offer lots of samples, and be able to buy quality, award-winning tea you won't find anywhere else in Taipei.

Tofu Ice Cream


Rating: 8/10 | Price: $1.70 | Location: 陳記豆腐養生恬點 @ Shengkeng, Taipei
If you're in Shengkeng, its worth the experience, but it tastes exactly what you expect it to be.

Central Market Seafood

Rating: 6/10 | Price: $18-30 | Location: Central Market Stir-Fry @ Zhongshan, Taipei

We saw a lot of what I can only assume are office workers that came here to eat and drink for dinner. Part of our order included shrimp and vegetable stir fry, squid and pork stir fry, and pork blood cake. It's very local and the food is decent!

Pepper Buns


Rating: 9/10 | Price: $2.10 | Location: Fuzhou Pepper Buns @ Raohe Night Market, Taipei
This place is very busy, and really is the only stall in all of Raohe that has a line. We got lucky and got our food in about 5 minutes, but a 30 minute wait has also not been unheard of. This place does indeed live up to the hype. It is crispy, fresh, hot, steamy, and peppery with a hint of sweetness from the onions. This is absolutely a must in Raohe Night Market.

Guabao


Rating: 8/10 | Price: $80 | Location: 林媽媽刈包 @ Raohe Night Market, Taipei
The taste and texture of the bun is very similar to a steamed bun, and contains two fatty pieces of braised pork, cilantro, peanuts, mustard greens, and some chili oil.

Peanut Ice Cream Roll


Rating: 9/10 | Price: $2.20 | Location: Raohe Night Market, Taipei
The roll has the consistencey and taste like a thin crepe, which is then filled with lots of peanut bits, cilantro, and two scoops of ice cream. None of this should work together but it actually tastes incredible - it is sweet, salty, and super fresh. This is also a must try in Raohe Night Market.

Street Corn


Rating: 2/10 | Price: $2.70 | Location: Raohe Night Market, Taipei
The flavors are nice, and is similar to popcorn chicken seasoning. However, the corn is dryer than beef jerky, while the cob has the consistency of beef jerky. Yes, the cob itself has somehow cooked to the point that I bit through and ate it without noticing.

Egg wrapped in Fishcake


Rating: 3/10 | Price: 5 sticks for $3.30 | Location: Raohe Night Market, Taipei
This is a quarter of a quail egg wrapped with fishcake, deep fried. It's very oily since it's soaked in oil for quite a while.

Tofu Pudding with Boba, Kidney Beans, Grass Jelly, Ai-yu


Rating: 4/10 | Price: $2.70 | Location: 鄉淳布丁豆花 @ Raohe Night Market, Taipei
Nothing special. The ice above the tofu pudding was very coarse.

Thick Shaobing with Egg, Thin Shaobing with Youtiao, Soy Drink, Peanut Rice Drink


Rating: 9/10 | Price: $3-5 | Location: Fuhang Soy Milk @ Zhongzheng, Taipei
The shaobings are both phenomenal, but I prefer the thin one so it tastes less bready. Shaobing is pretty common in Taipei, so these items are slightly higher quality than a lot of already available competition, but people love to line up for it. I was in line for an hour. I also got the soy milk (pretty standard) and peanut rice drink, which is sweet and decently starchy due to the rice. It's kind of like drinking peanut butter, in a good way. I didn't get to try it this time, but Fuhang is also famous for their salty soy milk which contains shrimp.

Pork Sandwich and Black Tea


Rating: 5/10 | Price: $1.70 | Location: Mos Burger @ Everywhere
Pork sandwich was kind of disappointing, but filling. However, the black tea is amazing. I didn't expect black tea from a fast food place to be so good, and if it weren't for the plethora of drinks available anywhere I would most definitely come back for the tea.

Meat-stuffed Tofu and Dongfen Noodles


Rating: 9/10 | Price: $2.30 | Location: Fishball Bonzai @ Jioufen, New Taipei City
This is unique to Jioufen and can't be found anywhere else. The tofu is stuffed with spicy meat, and sealed with fishcake and simmered. It has a slightly sweet sauce and is decently spicy. The dongfen noodles are also very bouncy and are fragrant because of the onions, green onions, and red garlic.

Conveyer Belt Sushi






Rating: 7/10 | Price: $40 | Location: Sushiro Sushiro @ Zhongshan, Taipei
Very solid, fresh sushi. However, they don't have an english menu available.

Beef Bourginoun, French Tea, Chocolate Latte, and Fruit Layer Cake UPDATE LOCATION



Rating: 5/10 | Price: $23 | Location: Mr. Chu @ Yonghe, New Taipei City
This is a cat cafe, so the higher prices are to be expected. The drinks and food didn't feel particularly special, but were still delicious. The cake had crepe-like layers, with kiwi, dragonfruit, orange, and banana. I would recommend trying somewhere else.

Taro, Sweet Potato, and Mung Bean Balls


Rating: 9/10 | Price: $2.00 | Location: A Gan Yi Taro Ball @ Jioufen, New Taipei City
These are significantly difference in nearly every single way compared to other stores. The balls are incredibly bouncy, but in a way that seems closer to something like a chewy jelly, compared to other balls that always that are starchy and gritty. Nesting below the balls is coarse ice, mung beans, and red beans.

Jin Xuan Tea and Tea Snacks



Rating: 8/10 | Price: $20 + $3.30pp for tea, ~$3.30 per snack | Location: A-Mei Teahouse @ Jioufen, New Taipei City
The Jin Xuan tea is light and refreshing, but consuming too much can cause an upset stomach which is why tea snacks must be eaten along with the tea. The tea also smells like butter and does have hints of butter in taste for the first two steeps. The tea snacks we had were dried squid, edamame, and mung bean cake. The mung bean cake is spectacular - it is very sweet, sandy (which cakes the roof of your mouth), and has a pretty negligible amount of red bean inside. The cake pairs super well with tea.

Chocolate Latte


Rating: 6/10 | Price: $5 | Location: Coffee Law @ Da'an, Taipei
Solid chocolate milk! Ambience is fantastic. They are known for their watermelon coffee, but I don't drink coffee unfortunately.

Steamed Meat Bun


Rating: 8/10 | Price: $0.50 | Location: Yilan Gold Medal Buns @ Yingge Old Street, New Taipei City
The most freshly steamed meat buns served ever. I had to wait 20 minutes before attempting to eat it. The buns are perfectly pillowly, lightly sweet, and has a peppery filling. The inside also holds a lot of soup/meat juice.

Lemon Ai-yu Drink


Rating: 5/10 | Price: <$1 | Location: Yingge Old Street, New Taipei City
Kind of like a lemonade, but with tasteless jelly inside.

"Sushi" and Garlic Noodles


Rating: 3/10 | Price: $10 | Location: 阿婆壽司 @ Yingge Old Street, New Taipei City
This isn't poorly rated because the food tasted bad, but rather, it was an odd combination and confusing eating experience. First off, the tofu bags are surprisingly sweet. I usually love eating tofu bags but these are insanely sweet. The roll wrapped in egg contains rice, cucumber, mayo, pork floss, and ham. This seriously shouldn't work, and it almost does. The mayo is just way too sweet. The seaweed roll contains rice, pork floss, and some kind of crunchy melon. This was my favorite here. The garlic noodles were insanely garlicky - I thought it was super spicy when I first tasted it, only to find out it was just pure garlic flavor. Apparently, it's made with red garlic which gives it its unique kick. Alongside the noodles came some mushroom and a century egg, both had no flavor.

Taro Duck


Rating: 7/10 | Price: $13 | Location: Tsui Feng Yuan @ Songshan, Taipei City
I found this place because it had a Michelin recommendation. When I arrived, it was only elderly eating here, at which point I knew this was going to be good. Additionally, while I was waiting for my takeout, the waiter was super nice and gave me a cup of tea. The dish contains a thin layer of duck, covered in taro paste, then deep fried. Usually, this dish takes more than a day to prepare, and the price reflects the work needed to put into it. It is crunchy, savory, and to be honest, a bit too oily, but is otherwise a fantastic concept and delicious. It comes with some cabbage and dipping sauce. The dipping sauce has no flavor.

Grilled Fried Chicken


Rating: 4/10 | Price: $3 | Location: 維縈家鄉碳烤雞排 @ Zhongshan, Taipei
Taiwan is also famous for frying a chicken cutlet, smothering it in sauce, then grilling the whole thing for some char. I originally tried one in the states to find out that the quality wasn't great. Here, the quality is amazing, just the sauce is way too sweet to be enjoyable.

Breakfast Sandwiches


Rating: 4/10 | Price: $4.70 | Location: My Warm Day @ Zhongzheng, Taipei
I was craving some american food and was dissapointed to find out these sandwiches were sweet. The pancake sandwich (egg, hashbrown) tasted like pound cake, and the danish sandwich (tomato, egg, mayo, bacon) was at least better but still surprisingly sweet.

Smoked Chicken Bread and Garlic Danish



Rating: 4/10 | Price: $2.80 | Location: Sunmerry @ Da'an, Taipei
Solid, but still too sweet. Garlic bread was very garlicky and had good texture though!

Almond Berry Croissant



Rating: 9/10 | Price: $4.80 | Location: Muhei Bakery @ Da'an, Taipei
This bakery has both regular (butter, chocolate, etc) croissants and very odd (matcha, peanut, crab) croissants. The almond berry croissant had the perfect bready-flaky consistency, and was filled with chocolate and jam inside, but somehow wasn't overly sweet. I loved every bite of it! There was also a nice seating area inside, but I didn't stick around.

Cafeteria Food


Rating: 1/10 | Price: $7 | Location: Just don't go
There are quite a few locals, but the food is just so unbelievably subpar. The fried food spareribs were soggy, the fish was fishy, the vegetables and tofu were at least standard. However, cleanliness is horrific. One of the ladies that ladled food onto our tray dropped a sparerib on the counter, the one that had everyone's trays and hands sliding across it, and she put it back into the sparerib container. Actually disgusting.

Lemon Cake


Rating: 7/10 | Price: $4.20 | Location: SheMe Teahouse @ Beitou, Taipei
Great cake! It's a lemony pound cake with crunchy icing on top. They only allow 90 minutes to sit down though.

Sugar Cane Juice


Rating: 7/10 | Price: ?? | Location: Shilin Night Market @ Shilin, Taipei
Sorry, don't remember the price. Sugar cane juice is always good! It is fragrant, and very natural tasting.

Danbing, Youtiao, Pork Roll, Soy Milk


Rating: 8/10 | Price: $7 | Location: 永和豆漿大王 @ Da'an Taipei
This rating is based off of everything here except the pork roll. It is supposed to be flaky and salty with the pork floss wrapped in pastry, but it was dryer than eating straight up flavor and had less flavor than flour. However, the youtiao was freshly fried, fragrant, and juicy (because of the oil - very bad for health, but delicious). The soy milk was also fresh. However, the star was the danbing, an egg rolled into a wrapper. I can't tell if it's been pan fried or steamed, but the wrapper maintains a super bouncy texture despite being fairly thick. Paired with soy sauce and vinegar, this is a fantastic dish.

Chien-Yen


Rating: 7/10 | Price: $18.30 | Location: Chien-Yen @ Tamsui, New Taipei City
This is a buffet hot pot place, and the price is for a single adult lunch. Going in initially it felt grand (locals say its mid though). They have somwhere around 10 sauces, 8 drink machines (each with multiple options), a cooler with drinks (including beer), open fridges for meats, veg, mushroom, fishcake, balls, seafood, and noodles, a meat slicing bar with several different kinds to choose from, about 20 hot dishes (dim sum, stir fried, fried, steamed, grilled), a full dessert cabinet (cakes, etc), and a freezer for scooping ice cream and ice cream bars. This would have gotten a significantly higher rating if the meat wasn't sliced super thick, were super cheap cuts, and had cartilage. I would say that this could get an additional point not including meat.

Lu Rou Fan


Rating: 10/10 | Price: $1.60 | Location: Braised Pork Rice @ Zhongzheng, Taipei
This is what Taiwan's all about. It is pretty much 80% of just fat dumped on rice, but the way they braise it beforehand is legendary and isn't reproduced anywhere else. It is a simple dish, with massive flavor. It is slightly sweet and very savory. Although the amount of meat they give doesn't look like a lot, it is actually perfect given how fatty the dish is, which requires more rice per bite, but not in a bad way.

Honey Waffle and Elderflower Smoothie


Rating: 8/10 | Price: $13 | Location: Oromo Cafe @ Zhongzheng, Taipei
I kind of just wanted a place to sit down and do work, and had to order minimum a drink. I chose what seemed like two very normal dishes that should have come in a small plate and glass, and when I went down to pick up my food I couldn't believe what I just ordered. To be perfectly fair, both the drink and waffles aren't overly sweet, and they do have a lot of fun snacks to pick up on the side. However, this is not a light snack. Although delicious, do come here only when very hungry, and perhaps order some savory dishes - most people were ordering food and not sweets.
