The Casual Photography Experience

I've been holding on to a DSLR for the longest time, but never got around to learn how to use it. It sat around the house for about 6 years before I eventually picked it back up, and started taking some very amateur shots. Now, I actually do feel as if learning photography has motivated me to go outdoors spontaneously and often, like one time where I ended up in Gilroy and eventually went hiking in Uvas Canyon just for some photos (pretty underwhelming though).



The camera I'm using is the Canon D3300, bought from Costco. It has a 24.2MP sensor and came with a 18-55mm and 55-200mm lens. All of the pictures below were taken with the 18-55mm lens. Especially for amateur photography, I'm really grateful it also has autofocus and lots of presets. However, I've been trying to challenge myself with being able to work with manual mode and adjusting the exposure, shutter speed, and iso without any crutches.



Here's a photo I took near Steven's Creek Reservoir where I was just passing through to get to the reservoir. Even though I made a lot of noise pulling up to the parking lot and taking too many photos, the deer were relatively unfazed by my presence. This was my favorite of many other underexposed, blurry images when I maybe picked up the camera and have been learning to use it for a week.



This is also another photo near Steven's Creek Reservoir where there was a clearing and small, dirt offroad where I could pull over and take a photo. Again, I still had a lot of issues with getting the right exposure, so the sky seems to be the main focus while the trees and are severly underexposed. I think could have been fixed if I had taken this picture at a better time of day.



The picture above and the following pictures below are all from Los Angeles. For the one above, this picture was taken relatively close to sunset on a heavily polluted day, which makes the picture seem like fog. I could have done more work on the contrast of the image so that you could better see the rolling hills.



This picture is taken not far from the one before, but instead is taken from the balcony at Griffith Observatory. With this picture, I could better see the city and red hue from the sunset since the image taken was perpendicular to the sun.



I was really excited to try some night photogrpahy - my dream is to go out into Death Valley one day and get beautiful images of the stars, and a city at night is a decent substitute till that can happen. However, there were issues with capturing the city lights without illuminating the pollution, hence the cloudy image. Next time, it would be better to try again after a rainy day.



This picture and all but the last picture below are taken at Huntington Gardens, in Pasadena. This one was taken near the entrance, but otherwise isn't particularly special photography-wise. I just liked how the little flowerbed had water through the middle of it.



On the desert garden side of Huntington Gardens, there's a small conservatory with cacti both from the new and old world. This cactus is one of the new world cacti that stood out to me because of how perfectly symmetric and rounded out it was, in addition to being on of the few that was fuzzy instead of prickly. Although the picture came out darker because of it, I was able to adjust the aperture and focus to emphasize the cactus' details.



Here are a bunch of golden barrel cacti. Just thought it was cool.



This one has got to be my favorite photo in this entire page. I love this picture because of how I emphasized features of a single flower in a massive patch of equally beatiful flowers, and somehow pulled it off despite the colors in the background looking very similar. One thing I regret about it is having a couple of other flowers in focus, rather than the single flower in the middle.



Here is the Chinese garden, which has quite a lot of shade. It was difficult finding a good angle to take a picture without having an underexposed pong or overexposed sky.



There were also lots of wildlife at the pond! We saw a turtle, several koi, and lots of ducks. They are not shown in the image. This is a bridge.



This picture is taken at me and my roomates' apartment at school. For some reason I decided to make my own chess, which was only possible because of the makerspace at school. The chessboard is laser cut and engraved, and the pieces are 3d printed. Although it hasn't been done yet, both will be covered in polyurethane coat for UV protection and water resistance. I was able to get this lighting by placing two candles close to the white pieces, having a large aperture, and having exposure time be close to 1 second. I had a hard time to take a picture of the black pieces as the foreground though, since they would barely be lit compared to the white pieces.
